Franklin Patrick Herbert Jr.

Author - Journalist - Ecological Consultant - Lecturer

Here's a time line of Frank Herbert:

  • 1920 - Born in Tacoma, Washington
  • 1938 - Moved to Salem, Oregon with his aunt and uncle to get away from a bad home life.
  • 1939 - He lied about his age to get his first newspaper job at the Glendale Star.
  • 1940 - Returned to Salem to work for the Oregon Statesman newspaper (now Statesman Journal) in a variety of positions, including photographer.
  • 1940 - Served in the U.S. Navy's Seabees for six months as a photographer during World War II, then he was given a medical discharge. He married Flora Parkinson in San Pedro, California.
  • 1942 - His daughter, Penny was born February 16th.
  • 1945 - He got a divorce from Flora
  • 1946 - While attending the University of Washington, he met Beverly Ann Stuart in a creative writing class. They married in Seattle, Washington on June 20th
  • 1952 - Published his first science fiction story in the April issue of Startling Stories called "Looking for Something"
  • 1954 - Published 3 more stories in issues of Astounding Science Fiction and Amazing Stories.
  • 1955 - Published his first novel called The Dragon in the Sea. During this same year he worked as a speechwriter for Republican senator Guy Cordon.
  • 1959 - With his wife returning to work full time, he was able to devote himself full time as a novelist and begins his research for Dune.
  • 1963 - After 6 years of research, Dune was finally finished, but it was broken up in to 2 part. The first part called "Dune World" was published over several issues of the Analog.
  • 1965 - The second part of Dune, "Prophet of Dune" was again published over several issues of the Analog.
    It was then rejected by nearly twenty book publishers.

    "I might be making the mistake of the decade, but ...."

    -- One editor prophetically wrote
    Chilton Book Company agreed to publish the complete text of Dune.
  • 1984 - His wife Beverly Herbert died on February 7
  • 1985 - married Theresa Shackleford and published Chapterhouse: Dune, which tied up many of the saga's story threads.
  • 1986 - He died of a massive pulmonary embolism while recovering from surgery for pancreatic cancer on February 11, in Madison, Wisconsin, age 65
